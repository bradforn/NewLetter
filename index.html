A Need For Change
 

It has become an expectation of Universities anymore to demand a ridiculous amount of money for an education. Believe it or not it wasn’t always this way. “A student who worked a minimum wage summer job in 1978 could afford to pay their full year's college tuition without taking out loans (Moreno).

The cost of college is now at an all time high, and students can’t keep up. A 12 accredited student at Oregon State is recommended to spend a minimum of 48 hours a week, outside of classes, studying or doing homework. This doesn’t seem so bad on it’s own, but when you factor in that most students have full-time, if not part-time, jobs, and have to sleep, things get a stressful rather quickly.

The financial burden of the cost of college affect students performance in their classes. In a survey conducted in 2012, two third of students don’t buy required textbooks for their classes resulting in one fifth of those student to fail their courses because of not being able to afford the required textbook (Florida, 2016).

The cost of college textbooks has been growing out of control since the mid 80’s. There are large variety of factors that are causing but there are a couple of main reasons that explain why. Education is evolving, the way we are taught and learn is entirely different than the way students were taught and learn back in the 80’s. Almost every textbook sold these days has an addon or two. Unfortunately, they’re not always optional to buy either. Another, explanation for the rise of textbook costs is textbook publishers; they’re out to make a profit. It is becoming common practice amount textbook publishers to require authors to come out with annual additions of their book. This is unfortunate because often times the information the textbook provides doesn’t become outdated on a yearly basis resulting in the author's changing a few sentences and republishing the entire book. Forcing students to buy brand new textbooks.

The amount of students going without the required textbook for their classes in only increasing. This can only mean that the rate of students failing classes will most likely increase making the cost of education grow event more out of control than it already is.

There are alternative that could help prevent this such as teachers using open source materials or even no textbooks at all. And OSU has started programs that offer instructor incentives to teach without tradition textbooks. Unfortunately, the result is students receiving less of a quality of education.

Currently, there is a negotiation happening to push etext as an option for all textbooks required for classes here at OSU. Whereas etext would still cost students. It would reduce the cost of textbooks as etext version tend to be cheaper than traditional textbooks.
